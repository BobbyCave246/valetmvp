The decision requested

Approve a staged pilot with a maximum total cash exposure of BBD $55,000, including startup capital, lease-up losses and contingency. Capital is released in gates. Full fit-out proceeds only after at least 30 confirmed customer accounts and 150 reserved bins. The retrieval fee is BBD $50 per customer retrieval order, irrespective of how many bins are delivered in that order.

01

Executive decision summary

Approve a controlled experiment with a stop-loss—not a speculative scale-up.

Store All Valet would deliver standard empty bins to a customer, collect them after packing, store them on racking in Wildey and return selected bins on request. Customers pay BBD $25 per occupied bin per month and BBD $50 for each overall retrieval order. The retrieval fee is charged once per order or service request, not once per bin.

At 85% occupancy, the pilot is expected to produce approximately BBD $3,100 monthly operating contribution before depreciation, tax and central corporate overhead. Break-even is approximately 56% occupancy. The six-month base-case cash exposure peaks at approximately BBD $44,000; a low-demand downside peaks around BBD $51,600. A BBD $55,000 ceiling therefore provides only a limited contingency and must be treated as a hard stop.

What approval does—and does not—mean

Approval authorises demand validation, setup and a six-month operating trial within the BBD $55,000 ceiling. It does not approve a 4,200-bin warehouse, multiple vehicles, geographic expansion or any full-scale profit forecast. Those decisions require a separate investment paper based on measured pilot data.

02

Problem, customer and proposition

The gap in the current market

Traditional self-storage works well when a customer has enough belongings to justify a room and can transport them. It is less convenient for a customer with only a few containers, no suitable vehicle or a short-term storage need. The proposed pilot tests whether a concentrated Coverley customer base will pay a premium for collection, secure storage and scheduled return.

Primary pilot customer

The primary target is a space-constrained renter or student with approximately three to eight bins, a defined storage period and limited transport. Secondary targets are decluttering households, seasonal residents and people between homes.

Pilot product boundary

  • One standard hard-sided bin SKU only.
  • No furniture, bicycles, oversized goods or loose items.
  • Maximum weight per bin to be set before launch.
  • No hazardous, illegal, liquid, perishable or inadequately packed goods.
  • Pickup and initial empty-bin delivery included in onboarding.
  • BBD $50 charged once per retrieval order.
  • Order includes one delivery and, for temporary retrievals, one scheduled recollection within the defined service window.

The price-to-product crossover must be actively managed

At BBD $25 per bin, customers with larger bin counts may be better served by a conventional Store All unit. The pilot should not force a valet sale where a unit is cheaper or operationally simpler. Accounts consistently above approximately eight to ten bins should be offered a comparison and migration path.

Break-even

Estimated contribution before fixed cost is approximately BBD $21 per occupied bin per month. Fixed operating cost therefore requires about 280 occupied bins, or approximately 56% of capacity, to break even.

Payback

Base startup capex alone pays back in approximately 11 months at steady state. Including the expected lease-up losses, cumulative base-case cash payback is closer to month 19 from launch.

What drives the pilot economics

The BBD $50 retrieval fee is charged once per customer order, irrespective of the number of bins included. At steady state this produces approximately BBD $644 per month of retrieval revenue. The principal economic engine is therefore monthly storage revenue, while the retrieval fee contributes toward the cost of the delivery and handling service.

06

Operating design and capacity controls

Required warehouse zones

  • Empty-bin storage and preparation.
  • Customer intake, inspection and photography.
  • Quarantine area for damaged, wet or prohibited goods.
  • Active racking with barcode locations.
  • Retrieval staging area separated by route/date.
  • Claims and exception holding area.
  • Secure scanner/printer and administration point.

Required premises validation

  • Supplier-confirmed racking layout and safe loads.
  • Actual bin dimensions and clearances.
  • Aisle widths, emergency egress and fire requirements.
  • Manual handling and maximum reach assessment.
  • Hurricane restraint/bracing.
  • Flood, roof-leak, humidity and pest assessment.
  • Loading access and van manoeuvring.

One crew member is a hypothesis, not a conclusion

The base case assumes one dedicated FTE and one van. The pilot must record paid hours, overtime, customer-service time, stops, kilometres, warehouse touches and failed visits. Monthly profitability should be recalculated using actual labour, even where the employee's salary is already included as a fixed cost.

Policies required before launch

Maximum weight and packing

Set a safe limit per bin. Customer remains responsible for internal packing; staff may reject unsafe or leaking bins.

Retrieval fee scope

The BBD $50 fee covers one order, one delivery and one scheduled recollection within a defined period. Extra or failed visits need a stated charge.

Claims and declared value

Set a liability limit per bin, optional enhanced cover, claim evidence and notification deadline with insurer and counsel.

Non-payment

Define failed-payment notices, access restriction, late charges, termination and lawful disposal/abandonment procedure.

Privacy

Define why contents photographs are collected, who can access them and when they are deleted.

No-show and cancellation

Set cut-off times and charges so failed customer visits do not become an unpriced logistics cost.

Recommended month-six decision rule

Scale consideration Demand is above break-even, customer churn and service quality are green, actual contribution is within 15% of the approved base-case model and no unresolved legal, claims or safety issue exists.

Extend / reprice Demand is above break-even but one or more order-cost, churn or service metrics are amber and have a credible corrective plan.

Stop Occupancy remains below break-even, cash is forecast to exceed BBD $55,000, claims/safety are unacceptable or the customer proposition cannot cover actual operating cost.

09

Risk register and mitigations

Demand below break-even

Mitigation: require confirmed reservations before full setup; install modularly; stop if month-six occupancy is below 56% without a credible pipeline.

Price resistance

Mitigation: test exact BBD $25/$50 pricing before fit-out; measure conversion by bin basket and customer type.

Large multi-bin orders

Mitigation: measure bins per order and van capacity; reserve the right to introduce order-size limits or tiered fees after the pilot.

Peak scheduling pressure

Mitigation: appointment windows, route batching, order cut-offs and temporary vehicle/labour backup for semester peaks.

Damage, theft or chain-of-custody failure

Mitigation: barcode scans, tamper seals, CCTV, restricted access, claims process and broker-confirmed insurance.

Premises unsuitable

Mitigation: rack design, fire/safety, flood, humidity, pest and loading assessment before signing an unconditional lease.

Technology failure

Mitigation: offline scan process, daily backup, immutable audit history and manual recovery procedure.

Cash ceiling breached

Mitigation: monthly rolling forecast; no contingency release without named variance; automatic escalation before BBD $50,000 is committed.

10

Recommendation and immediate actions

Proceed, but make every dollar conditional on evidence.

The business case supports a pilot because the potential strategic benefit is meaningful and the downside can be bounded. A larger rollout should be considered only after demand, workload, service quality and physical capacity have been proven through the pilot.

Recommended resolution

Approve the Coverley valet storage pilot subject to a maximum total cash exposure of BBD $55,000; demand-validation gates of 30 confirmed accounts and 150 reserved bins; completion of premises, insurance, legal and safety due diligence; monthly reporting against the defined KPIs; and a formal go/extend/stop decision at month six. Any full-scale expansion requires a separate business case.

First 30-day work programme

  1. Confirm product rules, bin size, maximum weight, retrieval window and customer terms.
  2. Obtain firm quotes for bins, racking, insurance, van and WMS.
  3. Complete a scaled rack and workflow layout for the actual Wildey premises.
  4. Launch a pricing page and reservation campaign to Coverley and Ross-linked audiences.
  5. Take deposits or confirmed start dates and record requested bin counts.
  6. Return to management at Gate 0 before committing the full fit-out.
