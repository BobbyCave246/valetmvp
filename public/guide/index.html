📦 Store All Valet — how it works

Staff sign in

Store All Valet is storage that comes to you. Customers never visit the warehouse: we deliver empty bins to their door, they fill them, we collect, store, and bring them back whenever asked. This page walks the full journey of a bin — who does what, on which screen, at every step.

The four roles

🙋 Customer CUSTOMER Books bins, photographs contents, tracks their bins, requests them back. Uses their own phone — no app install, no login (lookup by phone number or booking reference).
Open the booking site ↗
🚚 Driver DRIVER Runs the day's deliveries, collections and returns. Sees customer name, address and phone per job, and scans each bin's barcode at handover to confirm the right bins changed hands.
Open the driver app ↗
🏭 Warehouse staff WAREHOUSE Puts arriving bins away (scan bin → scan rack slot) and pulls requested bins back out. Every scan updates the live rack map.
Open the scan station ↗
🗄️ Ops / admin ADMIN Desktop supervisor console: live dashboard, booking queue, bin assignment, customers, dispatch, inventory view, and chain-of-custody explorer. Warehouse scans and job completion happen in the field apps.
Open the admin console ↗

A bin's journey at a glance

Booked→ Out for filling→ In transit→ Stored→ On its way back→ Back with customer→ Re-store or close

Every status change is enforced by a state machine — a bin can never skip a step or move backwards — and every change writes a timestamped movement record, so each bin carries a complete chain of custody from first scan to final return.

The full flow, step by step

1

Customer books bins CUSTOMER

We check their area is covered, they choose how many bins of each size, pick a delivery date and time window, and leave contact details. They get a booking reference straight away and can track everything from the "My booking" page.
System creates the booking and schedules a Deliver empty bins job.
2

Ops assigns physical bins ADMIN

One click auto-assigns matching free bins from inventory (or staff scan specific bins manually). From this moment each physical barcode is bound to that customer.
Bins: unassigned → Assigned
3

Driver delivers the empties DRIVER

The job appears on the driver's phone under "Today" with the customer's address and a tap-to-call number. At the door the driver scans each bin to confirm the handover, then marks the job done.
Bins: Assigned → Out for filling
4

Customer fills the bins CUSTOMER

They pack at their own pace, snap a contents photo per bin from their phone (so they can see what's inside later without opening it), and book a collection date when ready.
System schedules a Collect filled bins job.
5

Driver collects DRIVER

Same rhythm as delivery: scan every bin at the doorstep to confirm the pick list, mark done, and the bins are on their way to the warehouse.
Bins: Out for filling → In transit (inbound)
6

Warehouse puts away WAREHOUSE

As bins come off the van: scan the bin, scan the rack slot, done — the system rejects a slot that's already taken and the rack map updates live. The customer's tracker now shows "Safely stored".
Bins: In transit (inbound) → Stored (slot recorded)
7

Customer asks for bins back CUSTOMER

Weeks or months later, they tick the bins they want (any subset — the rest stay stored) and pick a delivery-back date.
Bins: Stored → Retrieval requested · system schedules a Deliver bins back job.
8

Warehouse pulls out, driver returns WAREHOUSE DRIVER

Warehouse scans the bin out (its slot frees up instantly), the driver runs it back and confirms the handover with a final scan at the customer's door.
Bins: Retrieval requested → In transit (outbound) → Returned to customer
9

Re-store or close CUSTOMER

The customer either sends the bin straight back to storage (no need to re-book from scratch) or closes it out. Closed bins are wiped of customer data and return to inventory for the next booking — their movement history is kept forever.
Bins: Returned to customer → back to In transit (inbound), or Returned / closed

Why this matters

For the businessHow the system delivers it
Nothing gets lost Barcode scans at every handover — driver at the door, warehouse at the rack. A bin can't be marked delivered, collected or stored without its barcode confirming it.
Full accountability Every bin carries a timestamped chain of custody (who moved it, when, to where). Any dispute is answered by the Bin explorer in seconds.
No illegal shortcuts The state machine rejects out-of-order moves — a bin can't be "stored" while it's still with the customer, and two bins can't claim the same rack slot.
Live operational picture The admin dashboard shows bin counts by status, rack occupancy and the job queue in real time as drivers and warehouse staff scan.
Low-friction for customers No account, no app install. Book on the web, track by phone number, photo-inventory from their own camera.
Cheap to run Staff use their own phones as scanners — the camera reads the same barcodes printed on the bins. No dedicated scan guns needed to start.

Try it yourself — 10-minute demo script

Best with two devices: a laptop for the admin console and a phone for the customer (the booking site) and staff (sign in at /login as the driver or warehouse account). Scan the barcodes shown on the laptop screen with the phone camera.

  1. CUSTOMER On the phone: book 2 bins for tomorrow on the booking site.
  2. ADMIN On the laptop: the booking appears in the queue — click Auto-assign bins.
  3. DRIVER On the phone: open the delivery job, scan both bins (off the laptop's Bin explorer), mark done.
  4. CUSTOMER Add a contents photo to a bin, then book a collection.
  5. DRIVER Complete the collection job with scans.
  6. WAREHOUSE Put away: on the phone, open Put away, scan each bin then tap a free rack slot (or scan the location barcode).
  7. CUSTOMER Request one bin back for tomorrow.
  8. WAREHOUSE Pull out: scan the bin on the warehouse app — the admin Inventory rack map frees the slot on the next refresh.
  9. DRIVER Run the return job, confirm with a scan.
  10. CUSTOMER Close the bin — then open Track this bin and show the full chain of custody, end to end.
Tip for presenting: keep the admin console on the laptop (queue + Inventory rack map) while phones run the field apps — watching the stats bar and read-only rack map update after each scan is the most convincing 10 seconds of the demo.

What's deliberately not built yet

This is a working prototype to validate the flow and data model. Before going live it would still need: payments and pricing, real accounts and staff logins, route planning for drivers, printed barcode labels, SMS/email notifications, and SiteLink (or other WMS) integration. The point of the prototype is that the operational backbone — the bin lifecycle, scanning discipline and chain of custody — is already proven end to end.
