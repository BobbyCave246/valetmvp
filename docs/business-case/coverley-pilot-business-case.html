The decision requested

Approve a staged pilot with a maximum total cash exposure of BBD $72,000, including complete startup capital, launch marketing, van setup, lease-up losses and contingency. Capital is released in gates. Full fit-out proceeds only after at least 30 deposit-backed customer accounts and 150 reserved bins. 01

Executive decision summary

Approve a controlled experiment with a stop-loss—not a speculative scale-up.

Store All Valet would deliver standard empty bins to a customer, collect them after packing, store them on racking in Wildey and return selected bins on request. Customers pay BBD $25 per occupied bin per month and BBD $50 for each overall retrieval order. The retrieval fee is charged once per order, not once per bin.

At 85% occupancy, the pilot is expected to produce approximately BBD $3,100 monthly cash operating contribution before depreciation, tax and central corporate overhead, after a BBD $250 monthly surge/overtime allowance. Break-even remains approximately 56% occupancy on fixed cash cost. The six-month base-case cash exposure peaks at approximately BBD $52,000; a low-demand downside peaks around BBD $64,000. The prior BBD $55,000 ceiling was too thin once marketing, security, van setup and onboarding burn were included; BBD $72,000 is the revised hard stop.

What approval does—and does not—mean

Approval authorises demand validation, setup and a six-month operating trial within the BBD $72,000 ceiling. It does not approve a 4,200-bin warehouse, multiple vehicles, geographic expansion or any full-scale profit forecast. Month-6 “scale consideration” also requires a forecast path to cumulative cash recovery within 24 months of launch, so occupancy alone cannot trigger expansion.

02

Problem, customer and demand sizing

The gap in the current market

Traditional self-storage works well when a customer has enough belongings to justify a room and can transport them. It is less convenient for a customer with only a few containers, no suitable vehicle or a short-term storage need. The proposed pilot tests whether a concentrated Coverley customer base will pay a premium for collection, secure storage and scheduled return.

Primary pilot customer

The primary target is a space-constrained renter or student with approximately three to eight bins, a defined storage period and limited transport. Secondary targets are decluttering households, seasonal residents and people between homes.

Pilot product boundary

  • One standard hard-sided bin SKU only.
  • No furniture, bicycles, oversized goods or loose items.
  • Maximum weight 25 kg per bin Template pending H&S confirmation.
  • No hazardous, illegal, liquid, perishable or inadequately packed goods.
  • Empty-bin delivery and filled-bin collection included in onboarding (costed below).
  • BBD $50 charged once per retrieval order.
  • Order includes one delivery and, for temporary retrievals, one scheduled recollection within 14 days Template.

What this means for the pilot

While one FTE has spare capacity, temporary retrievals can still be cash-positive on a marginal basis. On a fully loaded basis they are subsidised by monthly storage revenue. At steady state (~12.9 orders/month), the loaded subsidy is roughly BBD $600/month. The pilot must measure actual stop times and either (a) accept storage subsidising service quality, (b) raise the temporary-retrieval fee (e.g. toward BBD $75–100), or (c) charge for extra stops outside the 14-day window.

Labour accounting (no double-count)

In the cash P&L

  • The 1 FTE loaded salary sits entirely inside fixed cost (BBD $3,200/month).
  • A separate BBD $250/month surge/OT allowance is included in the base contribution so peaks do not silently erase profit.
  • Warehouse “touch costs” are not added again as cash on top of salary.

In capacity planning

  • Fully loaded stop/touch rates are used to test whether one FTE saturates.
  • When forecast loaded hours exceed ~85% of available hours, surge spend is activated.
  • Single-FTE / single-van downtime: backup van rental allowance BBD $2,000/year held in contingency Template.

Break-even

Net contribution before fixed cost is approximately BBD $21 per occupied bin per month (after VAT and processing, before allocating retrieval subsidy). Fixed cash cost of BBD $5,900 therefore requires about 280 occupied bins, or ~56% of 500-bin capacity.

Payback

Base startup capex of BBD $43,300 alone pays back in approximately 14 months at steady contribution. Including lease-up losses from the ramp table, cumulative base-case cash payback is closer to month 22 from launch.

08

Operating design and single-crew controls

Required warehouse zones

  • Empty-bin storage and preparation.
  • Customer intake, inspection and photography.
  • Quarantine for damaged, wet or prohibited goods.
  • Active racking with barcode locations.
  • Retrieval staging by route/date.
  • Claims / exception holding.
  • Secure scanner/printer and admin point.

Required premises validation

  • Supplier-confirmed racking layout and safe loads.
  • Actual bin dimensions and clearances.
  • Aisle widths, egress and fire requirements.
  • Manual handling and maximum reach.
  • Hurricane restraint/bracing.
  • Flood, roof-leak, humidity and pest assessment.
  • Loading access and van manoeuvring (Coverley↔Wildey routing).

One crew member is a hypothesis — with a costed backup

Base case: one dedicated FTE and one leased van. Record paid hours, overtime, CS time, stops, kilometres, warehouse touches and failed visits monthly. Recalculate profitability on actual labour. Backup plan: pre-approved contractor list; backup van rental up to BBD $2,000/year from contingency; if loaded hours exceed 85% of available for two consecutive months, present a second-crew paper rather than silently burning OT.

Policy defaults before launch Template — counsel to confirm

Maximum weight and packing

25 kg per bin. Customer responsible for internal packing; staff may reject unsafe or leaking bins at collection.

Retrieval fee scope

BBD $50 covers one order, one delivery and one recollection within 14 days. Extra or failed visits: BBD $35 per additional stop.

Claims and declared value

Default liability cap BBD $500 per bin. Optional enhanced cover sold at booking. Claims require photos + seal integrity; notify within 7 days of delivery.

Non-payment

Day 7 notice → day 14 access freeze on retrievals → day 30 late fee → day 60 termination path under counsel-approved abandonment procedure.

Privacy (contents photos)

Photos used only for condition/claims; access limited to named warehouse/admin roles; retain 12 months after bin closeout then delete.

No-show / cancellation

Cancel ≥24h before window: free. Inside 24h or no-show: BBD $35 missed-stop fee (same as extra stop).

Recommended month-six decision rule (payback-aware)

Scale consideration Occupancy above break-even; churn and service quality green; actual contribution within 15% of base-case model; no unresolved legal/claims/safety issue; and updated forecast shows cumulative cash recovery within 24 months of launch.

Extend / reprice Demand above break-even but order-cost, churn, utilisation or service metrics amber — with a corrective plan (including retrieval fee change if loaded cost stays > fee).

Stop Occupancy below break-even without credible pipeline; cash forecast exceeds BBD $72,000; claims/safety unacceptable; or customer proposition cannot cover actual operating cost.

This rule exists specifically to avoid a sunk-cost trap: green occupancy at month 6 is not enough if payback has slipped beyond two years.

12

Risk register and mitigations

Demand below break-even

Mitigation: hard Gate 0 deposits; sized catchment; modular 250-bin first install; stop if M6 occupancy <56% without pipeline.

Price resistance

Mitigation: test exact BBD $25/$50 before fit-out; measure conversion by bin basket and persona.

Temporary retrievals underpriced

Mitigation: measure loaded cost/order; extra-stop fee $35; mid-pilot fee review if loaded cost > $50 sustained.

Large multi-bin orders

Mitigation: measure bins/order and van fill; reserve right to tier fees after pilot.

Peak / single-FTE failure

Mitigation: $250/mo surge allowance; contractor list; $2k/yr backup van; escalate at 85% utilisation.

Damage, theft, chain-of-custody

Mitigation: scans, seals, CCTV, restricted access, $500/bin cap, broker binder before Gate 1.

Premises unsuitable

Mitigation: rack, fire, flood, humidity, pest, loading assessment before unconditional lease.

Cash ceiling breached

Mitigation: monthly rolling forecast; escalate before $65k committed; hard stop $72k.

Unit cannibalisation

Mitigation: crossover script at 8–10 bins; report diverted unit revenue monthly.

Insurance unavailable / too costly

Mitigation: binder is a Gate 1 prerequisite; reopen model if premium > $500/mo.

13

Recommendation and immediate actions

Proceed, but make every dollar conditional on evidence.

The revised case still supports a pilot: downside is bounded, Gate 0 is hard, costs are complete enough to steward, and retrieval/onboarding economics are explicit rather than hidden. A larger rollout remains a separate paper.

Recommended resolution

Approve the Coverley valet storage pilot subject to a maximum total cash exposure of BBD $72,000; demand-validation gates of 30 deposit-backed accounts and 150 reserved bins; completion of premises, insurance binder, legal and safety due diligence before Gate 1 capital; monthly reporting against the defined KPIs including loaded retrieval cost and FTE utilisation; and a formal go/extend/stop decision at month six that includes the 24-month cash-recovery test. Any full-scale expansion requires a separate business case.

First 30-day work programme

  1. Replace template catchment counts with estate / GIS figures; lock product rules (25 kg, 14-day recollection, extra-stop fee).
  2. Obtain firm quotes: bins (landed), racking, insurance binder, van lease, WMS, CCTV.
  3. Complete scaled rack and workflow layout for the actual Wildey bay.
  4. Launch pricing + reservation campaign to Coverley and Ross-linked audiences with deposit checkout.
  5. Record confirmed accounts under the hard Gate 0 definition only.
  6. Return to management at Gate 0 before committing fit-out, racking or bulk bin purchase.
